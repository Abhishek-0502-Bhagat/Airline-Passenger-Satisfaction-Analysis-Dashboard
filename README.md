# Airline-Passenger-Satisfaction-Analysis-Dashboard

This dataset tells a very real story about airline experience — people don’t just care about reaching their destination, they care about how the journey feels. Business class passengers are clearly the happiest (69% satisfaction), but what’s more interesting is that returning customers are far more satisfied (75%) than first-time travelers (24%), which means first impressions are not strong enough.

Another big takeaway is how sensitive satisfaction is to delays — even small delays start to bring it down. For example, satisfaction is around 47.5% with 0–5 min delays, but drops to 35% when delays go beyond 30 minutes. But the most surprising insight is that online boarding has the biggest impact on satisfaction (0.50), even more than things like food or comfort (seat comfort 0.35, cleanliness 0.31).

Overall, the data makes one thing clear: if airlines want happier and more loyal customers, they need to focus on smooth operations, better first-time experiences, and improving economy class (19% satisfaction). It’s not just about flying — it’s about delivering a consistent experience from start to finish.

👉 This is a fairly straightforward dataset, so I focused on maximizing insights through visualization and storytelling.
